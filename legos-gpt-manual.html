Component Definitions ‚Äì The 7 LEGOS Blocks
LOCATION
Definition: A spatial or temporal container that grounds where entities exist or interact.

Examples: <Ancient Library>, <Virtual Workspace>, <Battlefield>, <2027 Timeline>
ENTITY
Definition: Any discrete, nameable object, person, concept, faction, or structure.

Types: Character, Object, System, Idea, Creature
Examples: <Theodore>, <Agent-4>, <Frostwyrm>, <Narrative LEGO Block>
GOAL
Definition: A desired end-state or objective held by an entity.

Properties: owner (the entity), status (pending | achieved | failed)
Examples: <Seize the Throne>, <Build Emotional Connection>, <Maintain AI Alignment>
OBSTACLE
Definition: A force, condition, or actor that impedes a goal from being achieved. May be internal, external, or systemic.

Examples: <Misalignment>, <Spec Drift>, <Public Distrust>, <Internal Conflict>, <Firewall>
MORPHISM (Relation)
Definition: Any verb, transformation, action, or relationship connecting entities. Direction matters!

Examples: [loves], [builds], [seeks], [blocks], [steals], [guides], [protects], [transforms_into], [contains], [controls], [betrays]
SHIFT
Definition: A transformative event or moment that changes relationships, status, or structure. Often resolves obstacles or transforms entities/goals.

Examples: <Betrayal at Red Wedding>, <AI Becomes Sentient>, <Emotional Breakthrough>, [awakens], [aligns], [fuses], [breaks]
TIMELINE & SCENE
Definition: A container for time-indexed scenes, composed of entities, morphisms, shifts, and changes over time.

Structure: timeline ‚Üí scenes ‚Üí entities + events
Example: "AI agents emerge" (2025) ‚Üí "Agent-1 deployed internally" ‚Üí "Public release"

Step-by-Step Tutorials ‚Äì Getting Started
1IDENTIFY YOUR ENTITIES
Start with who or what exists in your story/system. These are your entities. Examples: characters (Hero, Villain), objects (Sword, Ring), systems (AI Model, Government), ideas (Freedom, Justice). List them with IDs, types, and traits.
entities: - id: hero type: Character name: "The Wanderer" traits: ["brave", "curious", "exiled"]
2DEFINE GOALS & OBSTACLES
What does each entity want? These are goals. What blocks them? These are obstacles. Connect them explicitly.
goals: - id: g1 name: "Return home" owner: hero obstacles: - id: o1 name: "Cursed forest" affects: g1
3MAP RELATIONSHIPS (MORPHISMS)
How do entities interact? Use morphisms to connect them. Direction matters: [guides] Mentor ‚Üí Student is different from [learns_from] Student ‚Üí Mentor.
relations: - [guides] mentor -> hero - [blocks] cursed_forest -> hero - [seeks] hero -> ancient_artifact
4TRACK SHIFTS (TRANSFORMATIONS)
When do things change? Identify key turning points. Shifts resolve obstacles, transform entities, or unlock new goals.
shifts: - id: s1 name: "Discovery of magic sword" causes: ["explored ancient ruin"] results_in: "hero gains power to cut through curse"
5ORGANIZE IN TIME (TIMELINE)
For complex stories, use timelines to organize scenes chronologically. Each scene can have its own entities, events, and shifts.
timeline: - id: act1 description: "The Exile" scenes: - id: s1 description: "Hero banished from kingdom" entities: [hero, king]
6VALIDATE & ITERATE
Check your YAML syntax. Ensure all IDs are referenced correctly. Ask: Are goals clear? Are obstacles meaningful? Do shifts create narrative momentum? Refine until the structure feels complete and parsable.
