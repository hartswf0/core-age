DAY 1 – THE PLAN

[00:00] I'm building a LEGO city. Not just any city—Brick Haven. A complete urban environment demonstrating long-horizon planning. 170 pieces. 5 districts. One MPD file. Let's break it down.

[00:15] The brief is clear: demonstrate that an agent can plan, coordinate, and execute a complex build across multiple phases without losing coherence. No overlapping structures. Perfect alignment. Each piece placed with intent.

The Master Plan

[01:00] First, I need a grid system. I choose 10x10 blocks at 120 LDU spacing. Total footprint: 1200 x 1200 LDU (~48 x 48 studs). This gives me room to breathe.

[01:30] Districts are the bones of the city:

  • Residential (North, Z > 0): Red, Yellow, Blue, White. 8 houses with yards. Families live here.
  • Commercial (Center, ±120): Dark Grey, Light Grey, Tan. 6 office buildings. Business happens here.
  • Civic (West, X < 0): Blue, White, Green. City hall + park. Government and green space.
  • Industrial (South, Z < 0): Dark Grey, Brown, Orange. 3 warehouses. Function over form.
  • Parks (East, X > 0): Green, Lime. Trees and open space. Recreation.

[02:00] Elevation strategy: Buildings range from 2-4 stories (48-128 LDU). City Hall will be the tallest—6 stories. This creates visual hierarchy. No monotony.

DESIGN DECISION: Color-code each district. This isn't just aesthetics—it's wayfinding. Residential is warm and welcoming. Commercial is professional. Civic is institutional. Industrial is utilitarian. Parks are natural.

DAY 3 – THE AGENT INTERFACE

[11:00] I hit a wall. Browser automation is flaky when trying to load MPD files line-by-line. The DOM is too complex. Clicking through dropdowns and modals is unreliable.

[11:30] The solution: build an API-in-UI. I add a hidden overlay to wag-grace-editor.html:

A simple textarea (agent-mpd-input) and a button (agent-load-btn). The agent pastes the full MPD content. Click load. Instant rendering. No flaky automation.

[12:00] I write the JavaScript API:

fetch('brick_haven_city.mpd')
  .then(r => r.text())
  .then(t => {
    document.getElementById('agent-mpd-input').value = t;
    document.getElementById('agent-load-btn').click();
  });

[12:30] Test run. Success. 200+ parts load instantly. The agent interface works. This is the bridge between automation and the UI.

BREAKTHROUGH – Brick Haven loaded via Agent Interface. 170 pieces rendered in under 1 second.
